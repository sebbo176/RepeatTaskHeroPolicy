Tropical Cyclone Alfred – Latest Forecast & Path for Victoria Point (QLD 4165)

Expected Landfall Location & Timing

Based on current meteorological data (as of March 3, 2025), Cyclone Alfred is projected to approach the south-east Queensland coast late Thursday. The Bureau of Meteorology (BoM) indicates a possible Category 1–2 landfall in the greater Brisbane/Sunshine Coast area on Thursday night or early Friday (March 6–7, 2025). However, there remains uncertainty in the exact track. Residents in Victoria Point should closely monitor official sources for updates.

Wind Speeds and Intensity Near Victoria Point

Cyclone Alfred may maintain Category 1 or 2 intensity at landfall, with sustained winds near the core of 90–110 km/h. Even if the cyclone’s center does not pass directly over Victoria Point, damaging wind gusts over 90 km/h could affect the region by Wednesday, potentially escalating to destructive gusts above 130 km/h by Thursday. Downed power lines, falling trees, and flying debris are possible hazards.

Rainfall Projections

Heavy rainfall is anticipated throughout south-east Queensland, including Victoria Point. Accumulations could exceed 200–300 mm, with some localities potentially reaching 500 mm or more. This rain may lead to flash flooding in low-lying areas and major flood warnings have been issued for certain catchments.

Official Warnings & Alerts

The BoM has placed southeast Queensland under a Tropical Cyclone Watch. Residents should prepare for conditions to deteriorate from Wednesday onward. Additionally, Flood Watches are in effect, and Redland City Council has urged people in vulnerable areas to consider evacuating early. Stay updated through official channels and follow any emergency alerts or evacuation orders immediately.

Recommendations for Victoria Point Residents

  • Secure Your Property: Bring inside loose outdoor items, close shutters or board windows, and park vehicles away from trees.
  • Prepare for Flooding: If you live near the coast or along waterways, develop a plan in case of rising water levels or storm surge.
  • Evacuate if Necessary: If your home cannot withstand high winds or potential flooding, leave well ahead of the approaching cyclone to a safer location or shelter.
  • Build an Emergency Kit: Stock up on essential supplies, water, non-perishable food, medication, first aid, and battery-powered devices to last at least three days.
  • Stay Informed: Monitor BoM advisories and local emergency broadcasts. Obey all instructions from authorities without delay.

By following these recommendations and keeping updated with the latest information, Victoria Point residents can reduce the risks posed by Tropical Cyclone Alfred.
